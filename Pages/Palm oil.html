Palm Oil

In total, 50 million tons of palm oil is produced annually, supplying over 30% of the world’s vegetable oil production.


According to the World Wildlife Fund, an area the equivalent size of 300 football fields of rainforest is cleared each hour for palm oil. This large-scale deforestation is pushing many species to extinction, and findings show that if nothing changes species like the orangutan could become extinct in the wild within the next 5-10 years, and Sumatran tigers less than 3 years.


Over 90% of orangutan habitat has been destroyed in the last 20 years, and as such, is considered “a conservation emergency” by the UN. An estimated 1000-5000 orangutans are killed each year for this development.
